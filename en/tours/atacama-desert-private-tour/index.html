Why Choose a Private Atacama Tour

The Atacama Desert is a place of extremes and extraordinary beauty. As the driest non-polar desert on Earth, it offers landscapes found nowhere else: salt flats that mirror the sky, geysers erupting at dawn, valleys that resemble Mars, and night skies so clear that the world's most powerful telescopes call this region home.

A private tour transforms your Atacama experience from remarkable to unforgettable. Your dedicated guide adapts each day to your interests and energy levels, whether you want to photograph the sunrise at Tatio Geysers, spend extra time exploring ancient petroglyphs, or simply linger over a sunset in Valle de la Luna.

With TOUREVO, you also gain access to experiences unavailable to regular visitors: private observatory sessions with professional astronomers, exclusive salt flat excursions, and connections to local communities who share their ancestral knowledge of this mystical landscape.

Sample Atacama Itinerary

A suggested 5-day private tour, fully customizable to your interests

Every TOUREVO itinerary is crafted around your preferences. This sample showcases the possibilities; your journey will be uniquely yours.

Day 1

Arrival & Valle de la Luna Sunset

Private transfer from Calama airport to your luxury hotel in San Pedro de Atacama. After settling in and acclimatizing to the altitude, your guide takes you to Valle de la Luna for a sunset experience. Watch the desert transform through shades of gold, orange, and purple as the sun sets behind ancient salt formations.

Day 2

Tatio Geysers & Machuca Village

Early morning departure to witness the Tatio Geysers at sunrise, when the cold air creates dramatic steam columns. Enjoy breakfast surrounded by erupting geysers, then visit the charming village of Machuca. Afternoon at leisure to explore San Pedro or relax at your hotel spa.

Day 3

Salar de Atacama & Altiplanic Lagoons

Full day exploring the Salar de Atacama, Chile's largest salt flat. Visit Chaxa Lagoon to observe flamingos, continue to the stunning Miscanti and Miniques lagoons at 4,200 meters, and stop at the traditional village of Socaire. Private picnic lunch with Andean views.

Day 4

Archaeology & Private Stargazing

Morning visit to the Pukará de Quitor fortress and ancient petroglyphs at Yerbas Buenas. Afternoon free to explore San Pedro's artisan markets. Evening private stargazing session at a professional observatory with an expert astronomer guiding you through the southern sky.

Day 5

Departure

Leisurely morning before your private transfer to Calama airport. Depending on your flight time, optional early morning visit to a hidden canyon or final exploration of San Pedro.
